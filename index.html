The story

Two countries, one way of paying attention

My B.Sc. in Agriculture (Hons) at the College of Agriculture and Allied Sciences, Baramati, took me through agronomy, horticulture, plant pathology, entomology, agro-meteorology, agricultural economics, animal husbandry, and biotechnology — a wide lens on how a field actually works, above and below the soil.

"The earth revolves because of agriculture — it is the past, present, and future."

An exchange year at Van Hall Larenstein brought me to the Netherlands and toward the systems that move things from field to shelf. What began as a curiosity about crops became a fascination with supply chains: the networks, the routes, the quiet inefficiencies nobody notices until someone counts them.

An MSc in Business Administration (Supply Chain & Operations Management) at TIAS gave that instinct a vocabulary: Lean Six Sigma, root-cause analysis, KPI design, data storytelling. Three-plus years across operations and analytics work later, I still think in the same terms I learned on a farm: what's the season, what's the constraint, and what needs tending first.
